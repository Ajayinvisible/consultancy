China

China is a large country in East Asia. It covers an area of 9.6 million square kilometers. The Capital City of China is Beijing. It has a state government led by Presidency with State Council, Central Military Commission, and National People Congress. It is the most populated country with a population size of 1.402 billion. Education in China began in 1980 when the Government allowed the establishment of the first private institution for higher learning.

There are state-run public Schools with nine years of compulsory education at the primary and secondary levels. The top cities for higher education in China are Shanghai, Beijing, Jiangsu, and Zhejiang. There is a range of academic programs for studies such as Chinese, Mathematics, English, Physics, Chemistry, Political Science and Physical Education are renowned here. Post-graduation education in China in disciplines of Automation, Nuclear power, energy resources, oceanography, nuclear physics, Computer Science, Polymer Chemistry, Polymer Physics, radiochemistry, physical chemistry, and biophysics are much more popular and in demand.

Chinese government formed National Key Universities namely, Peking University, Tsinghua University, University of Science and Technology of China, Renmin University, Beijing University of Agriculture, Beijing Normal University, Beijing Institute of Aeronautics, Beijing institute of technology, Beijing Medical University, Fudan University, Shanghai Jiao Tong University, East China Normal University.

The languages spoken in China are predominantly Standard Chinese with other forms such as Sino Tibetan, Kra-Dai, Mongolic, Austroasiatic, Indo-European, Yenisiean, etc. Famous Chinese people were Confucious Philosopher, Dr. Sun Yat Sen Politician, Mao Zedong, I.M.Pei Architect, Bruce Lee Martial Arts, Jack Ma Alibaba Founder, Jackie Chan, etc.
